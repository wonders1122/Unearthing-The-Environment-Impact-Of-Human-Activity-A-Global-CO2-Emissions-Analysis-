About Co2

CO2 emissions refer to the release of carbon dioxide gas into the atmosphere, typically through the burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and gas. Carbon dioxide is a greenhouse gas, which means that it traps heat in the atmosphere and contributes to global warming and climate change.

The primary sources of CO2 emissions are transportation, electricity generation, industrial processes, and residential and commercial buildings. The burning of fossil fuels for energy production is the largest contributor to CO2 emissions, accounting for about three-quarters of all emissions.

In recent years, there has been growing concern about the impact of CO2 emissions on the environment and human health, leading to increased efforts to reduce emissions through policies, technologies, and behavior change.To achieve these goals, countries are expected to reduce their CO2 emissions and transition to low-carbon and renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power.
